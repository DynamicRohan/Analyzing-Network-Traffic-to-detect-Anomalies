Detecting network anomalies using statistical hypothesis testing

A one-tailed Z-test automatically spots a DDoS attack hidden in packet counts — step by step, live and interactive.

1
The four-step detection pipeline
Step 1
Model normal traffic
Packets arrive as Poisson(λ₀=100). This is our reference baseline.
Step 2
Compute rolling mean
Every minute, take the sample mean x̄ over the last n=5 minutes.
Step 3
Run a Z-test
Compute Z = (x̄ − λ₀)/SE. If Z > 2.33, reject H₀ and alert.
Step 4
Take action
Alert the security team or auto-trigger rate-limiting on the source.
2
Statistical model and hypotheses

By the Central Limit Theorem, the sample mean of n=5 Poisson observations is approximately Normal — making a Z-test valid even though the raw data is discrete.

H₀ — null hypothesis (safe)
Mean packet rate λ ≤ 100/min
Assumed true until evidence says otherwise
H₁ — alternative (attack!)
Mean packet rate λ > 100/min
A volumetric spike — DDoS in progress
Z  =  (x̄ − λ₀) / SE       ← test statistic
SE = √λ₀ / √n = √100 / √5 ≈ 4.47  ← Poisson: σ=√λ
x̄crit = 100 + 2.33 × 4.47 ≈ 110.42  ← α=0.01, one-tailed Z=2.33
